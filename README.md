Project 1: Slack Smart-Notification Onboarding
What I built: A specialized setup screen for new Slack users that allows them to customize their notification preferences—such as Direct Messages only or all activity—before they officially enter their digital workspace.

Why I built it: To solve the problem of "notification fatigue." New users are often overwhelmed by alerts or miss important messages because of default settings. This gives users total control over their attention from the very first minute.

Tech Used: JavaScript, Node.js, Slack Webhooks, Vercel.

Code: https://github.com/ibrahimadiallo-byte/Slack-Notification.git
Deployment: https://slacknotification-ten.vercel.app/


Project 2: iCloud Notes Web Clone
What I built: A high-fidelity digital notebook that replicates the Apple iCloud experience, featuring a three-column layout and an automatic "invisible" saving system.

Why I built it: To eliminate "saving anxiety." I developed a background synchronization engine that saves every word automatically as you type, ensuring that no thought is ever lost if a user accidentally closes their browser or loses their connection.

Tech Used: React, CSS, Vercel, Debounced State Management.

Code: https://github.com/LubaKaper/iCloud-Notes-Clone.git
Deployment: https://i-cloud-notes-clone.vercel.app/


Project 3: The Social Sofa (Netflix Party Clone)
What I built: A collaborative viewing platform that synchronizes movie playback across multiple devices in real-time, allowing friends to watch films together with a built-in chat room.

Why I built it: To turn streaming from a solitary activity into a shared social event. I built a synchronization tool that checks every 5 seconds to ensure every viewer is on the exact same frame of the movie, regardless of where they are in the world.

Tech Used: Next.js, Supabase, Tailwind CSS, TMDB API.

Code: https://github.com/papesy384/Netflixclone.git
Deployment: https://netflixclone-pearl-eight.vercel.app/

Project 5: AeroStress (Wind Turbine "True Age" Tracker)
What I built: A predictive maintenance tool that calculates the "True Age" of wind turbines by analyzing the specific terrain—such as mountains versus flat plains—where they are installed.

Why I built it: To address a major blind spot in the renewable energy industry. Standard software treats all turbines the same, but those in mountainous areas wear out significantly faster due to turbulence. AeroStress identifies which units are at risk of expensive failure before they actually break down.

Tech Used: Next.js, Supabase, Node.js, Mapbox GL JS, USWTDB Data.

Code: https://github.com/jonelrichardson-spec/AeroStress.git
Deployment: https://aerostress.vercel.app
